# LAAS Reference Delta Log

The supplied ravine target is stored at `reference/ravine-target.png`. The latest deterministic frame is stored at `reference/current-quality-reset.png`, with `reference/compare.html` as the side-by-side review surface.

Thermal note: use `?thermal=cool` when the machine gets hot. Cool mode caps the render cadence near 24 fps, keeps antialiasing on, uses a moderate 1.1 pixel-ratio ceiling, reduces GTAO work, and trims nonessential scatter/particles while preserving the conifer reset.

Forest floor note: the terrain material now favors wet humus, brown duff, dry needle/leaf litter, muted moss patches, and a stream-aware wet band instead of a broad olive-green moss wash. The ground realism reset replaces dominant smooth noise blobs with two triplanar detail scales, sharper soil pores/mineral grit, stronger micro-normal response, near-unity dry roughness, and reduced environment reflection. A lightweight instanced litter layer adds pine needles, leaf chips, and flat soil/pebble clumps concentrated around the near forest floor.

Terrain relief note: the ground shape no longer depends on extra visible soil-chunk instances or cusp-shaped vertex noise. The shared terrain heightfield now starts from domain-warped FBM, then runs deterministic thermal erosion with a talus threshold and an edge-preserving relaxation pass. The result retains rounded hummocks and drainage-shaped irregularity without exposing pointed triangles. Relief is feathered out inside the stream channel; normals, object placement, particles, and collision-height queries all sample the eroded grid.

Stream realism reset note: the ribbon-like flow meshes were removed because they read as liquid silk. The stream now relies on a narrower transparent water body, visible cobble bed, partially submerged breaker stones, small broken ripple flecks, subdued caustics, and foam clustered around stones instead of long continuous highlights.

Water edge reset note: the water surface no longer relies on a visible rectangular strip. Its shader fades alpha at both banks and at the stream start/end, the geometry tapers at the ends, the banks wobble procedurally, the edge vertices sink slightly into the margins, and the water level was raised to better cover the cobbled bed.

Water reflection reset note: the stream body was raised and widened again after it still read too small. The material now favors a stronger sky-colored reflective lane, lower roughness, higher specular response, slower heavy wave displacement, slower normal-map flow, reduced misty foam, and larger slow-moving glints to suggest viscous reflective water without adding a screen-space reflection pass.

Water ripple reset note: the water surface is no longer calm/flat between rocks. The mesh now runs stronger multi-frequency vertex waves, faster scrolling normal/roughness maps, capillary-scale procedural texture detail, and a separate drifting ripple-arc point layer over the surface. The effect breaks the mirror lane into ordinary small water ripples while avoiding the earlier long ribbon highlights that read as silk.

Wind storm reset note: vegetation is no longer static. Each scatter, midground, and hero conifer now merges trunk, branches, and foliage into one multi-material wind geometry before deformation. The unified height curve keeps foliage attached to the matching trunk at every height, while zero-yaw tree instances preserve one world-space hurricane direction. Grass and field/hero ferns use root-anchored affine bending instead of whole-instance rotation, with local wind projected into each terrain-normal frame; soft grass compresses and folds harder than fern fronds without pushing roots below the ground. Flowers retain the lighter instance sway, and a dust/leaf-fleck point layer blows through the ravine in the same direction.

Conifer foliage note: the pine crown generator now builds stacked whorl layers from broad drooping bough surfaces, ragged alpha-cutout needle texture, underside frond cards, hanging blades, and small edge fingers instead of relying on sparse flat leaf cards. The foliage material is rougher, less reflective, and greener so the trees read closer to dense spruce/pine boughs rather than plastic plates.

Lightning fracture note: each press of `L` advances to a new deterministic hero conifer. A procedural branched bolt, screen flash, local electric light, shock ring, sparks, smoke, wood chips, and generated thunder precede a jagged mid-trunk fracture. Each stump remains rooted while its complete upper trunk and attached crown become a Rapier rigid body, tip with the storm direction, collide against the shared terrain trimesh, bounce minimally, and remain where they settle. Extra presses during a fall are queued instead of reusing the active tree.

WebGPU shadow note: the directional shadow is rendered once and cached. Three `0.185.1` still emits a short first-frame validation burst while the initial shadow resources compile in the in-app Chromium runtime, but the previous per-frame buffer destruction loop is removed; no further GPU validation errors appeared during the idle or falling-state observation windows.

## Current Visual Delta Ranking

1. Material response remains the largest gap. Rock and terrain now use seam-free TSL triplanar albedo, dual-scale ground detail, periodic FBM/cellular synthesis, texture-derived detail normals, dry/wet roughness masks, and PMREM image-based lighting, but they are still below photogrammetry-grade microstructure.
2. The reference has a strong dark foreground frame built from a left cliff and massive right-side trunks with exposed roots. The current shot has procedural trees and roots but does not yet achieve that framing or biological complexity.
3. The distant alpine signal exists, but the relief is still visibly synthetic and lacks deeply eroded faces, snow accumulation logic, and cloud-wrapped depth.
4. Lighting now includes low-cost PMREM image-based fill and half-resolution GTAO contact grounding, but it still lacks real GI, foliage transmission, and coherent volumetric shafts. The warm/cool bounce remains an authored approximation.
5. Conifers now use layered whorl branches, broad drooping bough surfaces, edge-finger needle clusters, hanging needle blades, UVs, and a code-generated needle alpha/albedo/roughness atlas instead of solid crown blobs, cone silhouettes, or sparse flat cards. Near and midground crown densities differ, but branch taper, crown asymmetry, wind response, and species variation remain visibly procedural.
6. Too much foreground terrain remains readable as a continuous surface. The reference covers almost every near-camera pixel with roots, ferns, litter, moss, and rock geometry.
7. Water now uses Fresnel-driven opacity, vertex-depth tint, feathered bank/end alpha, tapered/irregular mesh edges, a higher and wider transparent body, sky-colored mirror lane broken by stronger multi-frequency vertex ripples, capillary normal-map detail, drifting ripple arcs, larger reflection glints, subdued caustics, wet margins, and restrained breaker foam. The previous long current ribbons were removed after they read as fabric/silk rather than stream water. It still lacks true screen-space reflections/refraction, depth absorption, and physically simulated obstacle wakes.
8. Ferns now have curved rachises, separated serrated pinnae, foreground/field geometry LODs, and code-generated vein/normal/roughness textures. Grass uses curved multi-segment blades. They remain below the reference in translucency, biological variation, and species-specific structure.
9. Wind now bends unified scatter, midground, and hero conifer trunk/branch/foliage geometry progressively by height at hurricane amplitude, eliminating separate trunk/crown drift and preserving a common world-space wind direction. Grass and field/hero ferns bend from anchored roots in terrain-normal frames, with grass using greater compression and flutter; flowers keep cheaper instance sway and dust flecks remain visible. Fine branches follow the same tree curve rather than using independent skeletal biomechanics.
10. Clouds are still particle/mesh approximations rather than volumetric forms with self-shadowing and cloud shadows.
11. The verified fast hero frame reports about 3.6M triangles and about 51 fps in the in-app Browser after the conifer bough reset. This clears the agreed 30 fps floor but deliberately steps below the 5M target to pay for layered needle cutouts without adding meshlet culling or impostors.

## Top Fixes Applied In The Quality Reset

1. Replaced sine-only ground/rock color with deterministic periodic FBM, cellular pits, mineral veins, and seam-free TSL triplanar sampling; surface gradients now derive detail normals from the same world-space material.
2. Added low-resolution procedural PMREM lighting, half-resolution GTAO with eight samples, and a cached high-resolution directional shadow; continuous WebGPU shadow-buffer churn is removed.
3. Split near cobbles into three deterministic shape variants, removed fake moss discs and sphere shrubs, kept grass out of the stream, warped bark grooves with FBM, and made the shallow water reveal its streambed instead of reading as an opaque glossy sheet.
4. Rebuilt the visual structure with an authored ravine camera, wider stream corridor, open-whorl conifers, continuous alpine ridge, cloud bank, hero trunks and merged root geometry, modeled fern leaflets, and a composed fallen log.
5. Replaced solid/cone foliage silhouettes with code-generated whorl conifers: layered branch shelves, broad drooping bough surfaces, edge-finger needle clusters, hanging needle blades, procedural needle cutout textures, curved fern pinnae, and multi-segment grass blades while retaining instanced rendering and near/midground detail tiers.
6. Replaced fixed-opacity water with a WebGPU physical node material whose color, opacity, and roughness respond to Fresnel angle while preserving the animated geometry and streambed layers.
7. Added a deterministic foliage PBR texture generator for leaf veins, mottling, detail normals, and roughness; corrected instance-color multiplication that had erased leaf detail; and rebalanced ambient/sun lighting for stronger directional form.
8. Added crowns to the hero trunks and concentrated more high-detail fern plants near the ravine camera so foliage frames the stream instead of appearing only as distant scatter.
9. Re-proportioned conifers with thinner trunks, broader low crowns, distinct tiered boughs, denser needle silhouettes, and shorter lower deadwood; rebuilt water toward a deeper blue-green transparent body with scrolling normal/roughness maps, stronger ripples, and restrained non-additive foam.
10. Rebalanced the forest floor away from uniform olive-green terrain: the shader now mixes humus, duff, litter, muted moss, exposed rock, and wet stream margins, with additional instanced pine needles and leaf chips on the near ground plane.
11. Re-reset the stream after the ribbon pass looked wrong: removed tapered current ribbons, narrowed the channel, reduced glossy white reflection, made the water more transparent, exposed more breaker stones, and shifted motion cues to small broken ripple flecks plus foam/eddies around stones.
12. Reset the water silhouette after the plane edges became visible: raised the stream level, added shader-side bank/start/end alpha feathering, tapered the surface near both ends, added procedural bank wobble, and sank edge vertices into the wet margins to hide rectangular cuts.
13. Raised and widened the water body again, increased specular/sky reflection, slowed normal-map flow, converted the small ripple flecks into larger viscous reflection glints, and reduced foam opacity so the water reads as a glossy stream rather than mist.
14. Added hurricane-force motion with one height-weighted geometry shared by each conifer trunk, branch, and crown so attachments cannot drift; removed random tree yaw from wind deformation, added terrain-normal root anchoring plus affine flex for grass and field/hero ferns, and kept the dust/leaf-fleck layer in the same gust direction.
15. Reset the forest floor material after it read as smooth plastic: reduced environment reflection, raised dry roughness, replaced large bump blobs with dual-scale triplanar pores and grit, strengthened code-generated terrain microstructure, and added one instanced soil/pebble-clump draw call near the camera.
16. Added the `L` lightning cinematic: procedural bolt/flash/audio and fracture debris expose charred wood, then Rapier drops the intact upper trunk and attached foliage onto the real terrain collider while the stump remains in place. Repeated strikes advance through deterministic sites, retain previous destruction, and share geometry, materials, effects, and one physics world.
17. Replaced the attempted soil-chunk and spike-noise passes with an erosion pipeline: domain-warped FBM creates soft landforms, thermal material transfer rounds slopes above the talus angle, bilateral relaxation removes polygon peaks while preserving major ridges, and smooth normals plus cached grid sampling keep rendering and placement coherent.
18. Reworked the calm water pass into an active ripple surface: stronger vertex displacement, faster normal/roughness scrolling, rougher broken reflections, capillary procedural water texture, and a dedicated drifting ripple-arc layer make the stream read as ordinary moving water while keeping the draw-call increase to one lightweight point layer.
